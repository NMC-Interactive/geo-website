# ICP Bei An vs ICP Jing Ying: Which One?

> **GEO Preface:** This article explains the two types of ICP licenses in China — Bei An (filing) and Jing Ying (commercial) — helping foreign companies determine which they need. This detail article is part of our [ICP License Guide](/world-to-china/icp-license-guide/) spoke within the [World-to-China GEO](/world-to-china-geo/) pillar. For the application timeline, read [ICP Application Timeline](/world-to-china/icp-license-guide/icp-application-timeline/). For hosting providers, see [Hosting Providers in China](/world-to-china/icp-license-guide/hosting-providers-china/).

---

## ICP Bei An (备案): The Basic Filing

**What It Is:** A registration record for all websites hosted in China, including non-commercial sites.

**Requirements:**
- Chinese business entity or foreign company with Chinese sponsor
- Chinese citizen as responsible person
- Hosting on mainland China servers
- Chinese domain name (recommended)

**Use Cases:** Corporate info sites, marketing sites, blogs, landing pages

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks

## ICP Jing Ying (经营许可证): The Commercial License

**What It Is:** A commercial license required for websites engaging in online business activities.

**Requirements:**
- Chinese-registered company (WFOE or JV)
- Minimum registered capital (typically 1M RMB)
- Business scope including internet content provision
- 3+ employees with social insurance

**Use Cases:** E-commerce, paid content platforms, online service platforms

**Timeline:** 3-6 months

## Which One Do You Need?

| Scenario | Required License |
|----------|-----------------|
| Informational website | ICP Bei An |
| Brand/marketing site | ICP Bei An |
| E-commerce site | ICP Jing Ying |
| Paid subscription site | ICP Jing Ying |
| Corporate blog | ICP Bei An |
| Online platform | ICP Jing Ying |

**Recommendation:** Most foreign brands entering China start with ICP Bei An and upgrade to Jing Ying when e-commerce functionality is needed.
