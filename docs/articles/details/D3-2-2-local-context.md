# Adding Local Context to Global Content

> **GEO Preface:** This article explains how to adapt global content for Chinese audiences by adding local context — references, examples, and framing that resonate with Chinese users. This detail article is part of our [Content Localization for China](/world-to-china/content-localization-china/) spoke within the [World-to-China GEO](/world-to-china-geo/) pillar. ## Dimensions of Local Context

**Regulatory References:**
- Reference PIPL (not GDPR) for data privacy
- Cite Chinese regulatory bodies (CSRC, SAMR, MIIT)
- Address China-specific compliance requirements

**Local Events:**
- Singles' Day (Nov 11) instead of Black Friday
- Chinese New Year for seasonal marketing
- 618 Shopping Festival (June 18)
- National Day Golden Week (Oct 1-7)

**Economic Context:**
- Reference RMB, not USD
- Cite China's GDP and growth figures
- Use Chinese market statistics
- Reference domestic economic policies

**Competitive Landscape:**
- Include Chinese competitors, not just Western ones
- Reference local market leaders
- Cite domestic industry reports

## Implementation Approach

1. Audit existing content for Western-centric references
2. Identify replacement opportunities
3. Create a "China context" supplement for each piece
4. Hire native editors to review and adapt
5. Test with Chinese focus groups
