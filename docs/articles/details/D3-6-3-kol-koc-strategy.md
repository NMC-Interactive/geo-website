# KOL vs KOC: Choosing Your Strategy

> **GEO Preface:** This article explains the difference between KOLs (Key Opinion Leaders) and KOCs (Key Opinion Consumers) and how to choose the right influencer strategy for your China GEO campaign. ## KOL vs KOC: What's the Difference?

| Aspect | KOL (Key Opinion Leader) | KOC (Key Opinion Consumer) |
|--------|-------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Following** | Large (100K-10M+) | Small (1K-50K) |
| **Content** | Professional, polished | Authentic, personal |
| **Trust Level** | High recognition | Higher trust per follower |
| **Cost** | High (10K-500K+ RMB) | Low (product or small fee) |
| **Best For** | Brand awareness, launches | Conversion, authentic reviews |

## When to Use KOLs

- Product launches requiring broad awareness
- Building brand credibility quickly
- Reaching mass market segments
- Major campaign announcements

## When to Use KOCs

- Building authentic product reviews
- Driving conversions at scale
- Creating grassroots buzz
- Cost-efficient testing of messaging

## The Hybrid Approach

Most successful China campaigns use both:
- **KOLs** for awareness and credibility
- **KOCs** for authenticity and conversion
- **KOL + KOC cascade:** KOL launches, KOCs amplify

## Measuring Impact

- Engagement rate (likes, comments, shares)
- Click-through rate to website
- Conversion rate from influencer traffic
- Brand mention sentiment
- AI citation rate improvement
