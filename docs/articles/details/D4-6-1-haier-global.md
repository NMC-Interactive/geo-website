## Haier's Global Strategy

Haier, originally a Chinese refrigerator manufacturer from Qingdao, transformed into one of the world's largest home appliance brands through a distinctive global expansion approach.

## Key Strategies

**1. Acquisition-Based Expansion:**
- Acquired GE Appliances (2016)
- Acquired Candy (Italy), Fisher & Paykel (New Zealand), AQUA (Japan)
- Kept local brands and management (not rebranding to Haier)
- Leveraged acquired brands' distribution and reputation

**2. Localized Innovation ("Rendanheyi"):**
- Decentralized organizational model
- Local teams have autonomy to innovate for local markets
- Thousands of micro-enterprises within Haier ecosystem
- Innovation driven by local customer needs

**3. Brand Portfolio Approach:**
- Haier for mass market globally
- Casarte for premium segment
- Leader for value segment
- Acquired brands for regional markets

**GEO Lessons:**
- Local brands often have more credibility than global rebranding
- Decentralized content creation enables authentic localization
- Multi-brand strategy captures different market segments
- Long-term commitment builds lasting authority

## Results

- World's #1 major appliances brand by volume
- Present in 160+ countries
- 10+ global R&D centers
- Consistently cited as a case study in global expansion
