# Share of Model (SoM): Competitive Benchmarking

> **GEO Preface:** This article explains Share of Model (SoM) — the competitive benchmarking metric that shows your brand's share of AI citations relative to competitors. ## What Is Share of Model?

**Share of Model (SoM)** measures your brand's percentage of total citations received by your competitive set across a defined query universe. It answers the question: "Of all the citations AI gives in our category, what percentage go to us?"

**Formula:**
```
SoM = (Your Citations / Total Citations for All Competitors) × 100
```

**Example:** If you receive 15 citations and your top 4 competitors receive 85 citations combined, your SoM is 15%.

## Why SoM Matters

While Citation Rate measures your absolute visibility, SoM measures your **relative competitive position**. A high Citation Rate with a low SoM means you're visible but not dominant. A moderate Citation Rate with a high SoM means you're winning the competitive battle.

**Strategic Value:**
- Identifies whether you're gaining or losing ground vs. competitors
- Reveals which competitors are most successful at GEO
- Helps allocate resources to close competitive gaps
- Provides context for Citation Rate (absolute vs. relative performance)

## How to Calculate SoM

### Step 1: Define Your Competitive Set

Identify 3-5 direct competitors that your target audience considers when making purchase decisions. Include:
- Direct competitors (same product category, same target market)
- Indirect competitors (different approach to same problem)
- Market leaders (even if not direct competitors, they set the standard)

### Step 2: Use Your Query Universe

Use the same 50-100 target queries from your Citation Rate measurement.

### Step 3: Count Citations for All Brands

For each query, count citations for:
- Your brand
- Competitor 1
- Competitor 2
- Competitor 3
- Competitor 4
- Any other brands cited

### Step 4: Calculate SoM

Sum all citations across your competitive set and calculate your percentage.

## SoM Competitive Analysis

| SoM Range | Competitive Position | Strategic Implication |
|---|---|---|
| 0-10% | Marginal player | Significant investment needed |
| 10-20% | Emerging presence | Building momentum, need acceleration |
| 20-35% | Strong challenger | Competitive, aim for leadership |
| 35-50% | Category leader | Maintain and extend advantage |
| 50%+ | Dominant | Defend position, expand category |

## SoM Growth Strategies

**To Increase SoM:**
1. **Identify gap queries:** Find queries where competitors are cited and you're not
2. **Create competitive content:** Develop content that directly addresses comparison queries
3. **Accelerate authority building:** Fast-track authority signals where competitors are strong
4. **Target competitor weaknesses:** Create content in areas where competitors have thin coverage
5. **Expand query universe:** Target adjacent topics where you can establish first-mover advantage
