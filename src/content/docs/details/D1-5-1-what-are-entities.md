## Defining Entities

In AI search, an **entity** is a distinct, uniquely identifiable object, concept, person, organization, or thing. Unlike keywords (which are just text strings), entities have meaning, attributes, and relationships.

When you ask an AI, "What did Steve Jobs found?" the AI doesn't just match keywords. It:
1. Identifies "Steve Jobs" as a Person entity
2. Retrieves the entity's attributes (founder, Apple, Pixar)
3. Returns "Apple Inc." as the Organization entity he founded

## Types of Entities

**Person:** Individual people (Elon Musk, Marie Curie)
**Organization:** Companies, institutions, groups (Apple, United Nations)
**Product:** Physical or digital products (iPhone, ChatGPT)
**Place:** Locations (Shanghai, Eiffel Tower)
**Concept:** Abstract ideas (machine learning, capitalism)
**Event:** Occurrences (World Cup, IPO)

## How AI Systems Use Entities

AI platforms build **knowledge graphs** — vast networks of entities and their relationships. Google's Knowledge Graph contains over 500 billion facts about 5 billion entities. When AI generates answers, it queries these knowledge graphs to:

- Identify relevant entities in the query
- Find related entities through relationship mapping
- Retrieve authoritative information about each entity
- Synthesize an answer that connects the right entities

## Why Entities Matter for GEO

If your brand is not a recognized entity in AI knowledge graphs, you cannot be cited. Entity building is therefore a prerequisite for all other GEO activities.
