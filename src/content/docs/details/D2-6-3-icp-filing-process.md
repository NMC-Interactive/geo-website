# The ICP Filing Process Explained

> **GEO Preface:** This article provides a step-by-step explanation of China's ICP filing process — the mandatory registration for all websites hosted in China. This detail article is part of our [AI Regulations in China](/china-ai-ecosystem/ai-regulations-china/) spoke within the [Chinese AI Search Ecosystem](/china-ai-ecosystem/) pillar. ## ICP Filing Overview

ICP filing (备案) is the registration process required for all websites hosted on servers within mainland China. It is administered by the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT).

## Step-by-Step Process

**Step 1: Choose a Hosting Provider**
- Select a provider with ICP support (Alibaba Cloud, Tencent Cloud, Baidu Cloud)
- Purchase hosting within mainland China
- Obtain server IP address

**Step 2: Prepare Documentation**
- Business license (Chinese entity)
- Domain name certificate
- Responsible person's Chinese ID
- Photo of responsible person holding their ID
- Hosting service agreement

**Step 3: Submit Application**
- Register on MIIT's ICP filing system
- Complete online application form
- Upload all required documents
- Submit for review

**Step 4: Review Process**
- Hosting provider review (1-3 days)
- Provincial communications administration review (10-20 business days)
- Possible requests for additional information
- Final approval

**Step 5: Display ICP Number**
- Add ICP number to website footer
- Format: "京ICP备XXXXXXXX号" (for Beijing)

## Timeline and Tips

- **Total Timeline:** 2-4 weeks for Bei An; 3-6 months for Jing Ying
- **Tips:** Ensure all documents are complete and accurate; work with hosting provider for support; consider hiring a local agent
