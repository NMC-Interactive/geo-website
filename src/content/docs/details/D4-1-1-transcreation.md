# Transcreation vs Translation: The Critical Difference

> **GEO Preface:** This article explains the critical difference between translation and transcreation — and why Chinese brands going global must invest in the latter for effective GEO. ## Translation: Converting Words

**Translation** converts text from one language to another while preserving meaning as closely as possible.

**When It Works:**
- Product specifications and technical documentation
- Legal documents and contracts
- Company facts and financial data
- User manuals and instructions

**When It Fails:**
- Marketing copy and brand messaging
- Cultural references and humor
- Case studies with local context
- Emotional or persuasive content

## Transcreation: Recreating Meaning

**Transcreation** recreates content for a new cultural context, maintaining the intent and impact while adapting the expression.

**Example:** A Chinese case study about preparing for Singles' Day would be transcreated to reference Black Friday for a US audience — same intent, different cultural reference.

**When It Works:**
- Marketing campaigns and brand messaging
- Website copy and landing pages
- Case studies and customer stories
- Social media content
- Video scripts and advertising

## The Cost Difference

| Approach | Cost per 1,000 words | Quality for Marketing |
|----------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Translation | $50-150 | Poor |
| Transcreation | $200-500 | Good |
| Original Creation | $300-800 | Excellent |

## Recommendation

Use a portfolio approach:
- **30% Translation:** Technical specs, documentation
- **40% Transcreation:** Marketing content, case studies
- **30% Original Creation:** Thought leadership, research
